Overview

Choosing a bike that suits you can be a daunting task for new and seasoned riders alike. However, selecting a bicycle can be made easier by determining what type of riding you're interested in doing, the price range you're looking at, and the level of riding that you are currently at. With this information, selection of your next bike can be narrowed down to just a few options.

The Right Type of Bike

The first step in determining what bike to buy is figuring out what type of riding you will predominantly be doing, whether that be road racing, triathlons, or bike touring. Those interested in competing against others in races spanning from 20-100 miles will likely be geared towards road racing bikes. Anyone interested in biking similar distances for exercise purposes may also be interested in road racing bikes. Alternatively, those interested in participating and training for triathlons for leisure or at a competitive level may be more inclined to purchase a triathlon bike. However those interested in both road racing and triathlons should lean more towards road racing bikes as they can easily be used for triathlons while the opposite is not true. Riders who are not interested in racing in any capacity, but are interested in travelling long distances and carrying their gear while while they do so, or commuters who need a reliable way to get to work, a touring bike may be the best option available.

Determining Your Level

Figuring out what level you currently ride at is extremely important in making decisions that are financially sound and practical when it comes to choosing the right bike. Are you just getting into biking or are you a ten year veteran to the sport? Do you riding professionally or semi-professionally in a racing capacity? Is biking your main hobby or a side interest? Are you a triathlete or a road racer? All of these questions factor heavily into deciding what type of bike to buy. There are many questions that could be asked to help determine what level you are at as a rider. However the two most important considerations to be had are: what level cyclist are you (beginner, intermediate, or advanced), and how often/interested are you in cycling.

Selecting Components

Component selection is highly dependant on the level you are riding at and the type of riding that you are interested in doing. Newer cyclists, commuters, and other entry level riders will likely look at Shimano Tiagra components, while mid-level riders will look at Shimano 105/Ultegra or SRAM Apex/Force components. High level cyclists and racers who need the highest level of performance for durability or speed, will want to look at Shimano Dura-Ace or SRAM Red component sets. To help determine which component set is right for you, check out the SRAM component guide or the Shimano component guide.

Making a Purchase

Making a purchase is comprised of three basic steps: outline a budget, deciding what type of bike you're looking for, and determining what kind of components you're looking for. Once these things have been outlined begin researching bikes within these constraints and pick out 5-10 different bikes that seem like a good fit. Next try to find these bikes at local bike stores and take each one for a test ride. During the test rides be sure to ride on a variety of terrain (hills, flats, etc.) and get a feel for the bike. Finally when you have found the bike you're looking for, browse through various bike shops and online resources to find the best prices.
